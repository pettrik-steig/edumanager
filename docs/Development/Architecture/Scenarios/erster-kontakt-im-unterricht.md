# Erster Kontakt Im Unterricht

## Situation

A student appears in a concrete teaching situation for the first time from the
teacher's perspective.

This scenario is separate from the organizational assignment. The student may
already be assigned to a class, learning group, or course before the teacher
has the first real contact in class.

## Anlass

The teacher meets the student in Unterricht or prepares for the first lesson in
which the student is expected.

Possible triggers:

- the student appears in class
- the student is listed on a seating plan or class-use artifact
- the teacher wants to check whether the student's information is usable
- the teacher notices missing or uncertain information during Unterricht

## Real Situation

The teacher may use existing app output, such as a seating plan, during class.
This app output can make uncertain or incomplete information visible.

The first contact can lead to clarification:

- the student confirms information
- the student corrects information
- the teacher learns a preferred name or practical classroom detail
- the teacher notices that the assignment or seating situation needs follow-up

## Relation To Assignment Scenario

The first contact depends on prior assignment information, but it is a
different practical situation.

Assignment answers:

```text
Where does the student belong?
```

First contact answers:

```text
Can the teacher work with the student in the concrete Unterricht situation?
```

## Ergebnis

At the end of this situation, the teacher has practical first-contact
knowledge for classroom work.

Some information may later lead to app changes, but this scenario first
describes the real classroom situation rather than the data-entry process.
